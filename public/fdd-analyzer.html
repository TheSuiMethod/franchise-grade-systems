The FDD is designed to protect the franchisor, not you.

Hundreds of pages of legal language. Fee tables that hide escalation clauses. Territory definitions that sound protective but aren't. Here's what most buyers miss:

Hidden in Item 6

Fees that escalate with no cap

Technology fees, brand fund contributions, and mandatory vendor charges that can increase without limit. Your $250/month tech fee becomes $350 with 30 days notice. Then $500. No ceiling.

Hidden in Item 7

Startup costs that double

FDD estimates say $172K-$296K. Actual spend frequently exceeds $325K+ once leasehold improvements, architect fees, and real working capital needs are counted.

Hidden in Item 8

Vendor lock-in at premium prices

Required suppliers for bookkeeping ($400-$600/mo), software, insurance, and construction. You can't shop for better prices. The franchisor may have revenue-sharing deals with these vendors.

Hidden in Item 20

Franchisee exits they don't highlight

How many locations closed in the last 3 years? What's the turnover rate? The data is buried in Item 20 - and the franchisor is legally required to give you former franchisee contact info. Most buyers never ask.

Sample analysis output

Here's what a real Item 6 analysis looks like:

Item 6: Other Fees

HIGH RISK
RED FLAG
The technology fee has no cap on increases. The FDD states the franchisor "may adjust fees for required technology systems" with no maximum limit. This means your current tech fee could increase to any amount with as little as 30 days written notice - and you have no recourse.
Ask the franchisor: "What has the technology fee been for each of the last 3 years? What is the maximum it could increase to in the next 5 years?"
RED FLAG
A minimum royalty floor means you pay a fixed dollar amount in royalties regardless of revenue. If your business has a slow quarter, the royalty doesn't adjust down - creating significant cash flow pressure during the months you can least afford it.
Ask the franchisor: "Is the minimum royalty waived during temporary closures or force majeure events? What percentage of franchisees are currently paying the minimum vs. the percentage rate?"
CAUTION
Mandatory use of a franchisor-designated vendor for a core business function. This cost is separate from any independent providers you might prefer. Check whether the franchisor or its affiliates receive revenue from this mandatory vendor relationship (FDD Item 8).
Ask the franchisor: "Does the franchisor or any affiliate receive compensation from this required vendor? Can I use my own provider if they meet specified standards?"
